Boutique Coastal Retreat

Drift

Click to preview live site
drift-luxury-retreat.com

What It Is

Drift is a fully responsive, five-page brand website for a fictional luxury boutique coastal resort located on the Big Sur coastline in California. The site establishes a complete digital presence for a high-end hospitality brand, spanning a dramatic full-viewport homepage, an accommodations page with detailed room tiers, an experiences/activities page, an about page with brand philosophy, and a contact page with an inquiry form.

The design takes a serene, warm approach — cream backgrounds, sand-toned borders, a deep navy for dark sections, and a terracotta accent that evokes sun-baked cliffs. The typography pairs Cormorant Garamond (serif, editorial elegance) with Inter (sans-serif, modern readability). Every page builds a consistent story of exclusivity, sustainability, and personal service.

Why It Was Made

The project was created to establish a premium digital identity for a new boutique hospitality concept from the ground up. The core problem: how do you convey serenity, exclusivity, and architectural intention through a website — not just list rooms and rates?

The brief demanded a site that could tell a story: Drift is sustainably minded (solar-powered, locally sourced, plastic-free), architecturally intentional (only 12 keys, 2:1 guest-to-staff ratio), and deeply personal. Every design choice — from the warm coastal palette to the glassmorphism navigation — was calibrated to make visitors feel the tranquility of the property before they arrive.

Who It Was Made For

The primary audience is the affluent escape-seeker: aged 35–65, with high disposable income ($550–$1,800+/night), who values privacy, sustainability, and authentic experiences over flashy amenities. This persona is likely a couple or solo traveler looking for a digital detox — interested in wellness (yoga, spa), nature (surfing, hiking), and farm-to-table cuisine.

A secondary audience is luxury travel planners and concierges looking for curated partner properties. The site needed to communicate clear room differentiation, pricing hierarchy, and easy booking pathways — all while maintaining a tone of unhurried luxury.
