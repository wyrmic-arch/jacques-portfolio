Iron Row

What It Is

Iron Row is a multi-page marketing website for a fictional boxing and combat sports gym based in Chicago, IL. The site presents the gym as a gritty, no-nonsense training facility for everyone from first-timers to serious amateur fighters. It spans eight pages: a high-energy homepage with an animated punch counter, a filterable class schedule, detailed trainer profiles with individual bio pages, a three-tier membership calculator, and a testimonials carousel.

The visual language is aggressive and industrial — near-black backgrounds, bold red accents, all-caps Bebas Neue typography, desaturated trainer photography with multiply overlays. The entire aesthetic draws from fight posters and boxing gym interiors: raw, loud, and authoritative.

Why It Was Made

A combat sports gym needs to attract members in a competitive fitness market. The brief demanded a site that could do four things simultaneously: establish an immediate, aggressive brand identity; lower the barrier to entry for beginners (free trial, no contract); showcase credibility through detailed trainer credentials and real stats; and convert visitors into paying members through an interactive pricing tool.

The solution was a dark, cinematic hero with a live animated counter showing current gym membership — a subtle social-proof mechanism. Every page funnels toward action: "Start Your Free Week," "Book a Session," "Join Now." Trainer bios include amateur records and personality quotes to build trust. The membership calculator lets prospects build their own plan with add-ons and see the total update in real time.

Who It Was Made For

The target audience splits into three overlapping personas: the curious beginner who has never boxed but wants to get in shape (attracted by "No contract. No pressure." and the free trial); the weekend warrior who cross-trains and wants flexible hours, structured classes, and credible coaching; and the aspiring fighter looking for legit sparring, fight team access, and trainers with real records (14-0, Golden Gloves).

Geographically the site targets the Chicago metro area, with hours optimized for early-morning and after-work training crowds (5AM–10PM weekdays). The pricing tiers ($49/$89/$149) accommodate all three personas, from basic open-gym access to full elite membership with personal training and meal plans.
