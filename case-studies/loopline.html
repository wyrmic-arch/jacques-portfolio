What It Is

Loopline is a complete marketing website for a SaaS productivity and project-management platform. The brand positions itself as an "intelligent workspace" that unifies tasks, documents, and third-party tools into a single interface — the tagline reads "Work flows like it should."

The site spans five pages: a hero-driven homepage featuring an animated Kanban board mockup built entirely in HTML/CSS (no screenshot, no video — a live interactive demo), a detailed features catalog covering six core capabilities, a three-tier pricing page with monthly/annual toggle and full comparison table, an FAQ accordion section, and an about page with brand story and values. The aesthetic is dark-mode-first with a blue-violet gradient accent palette, reminiscent of modern developer tools like Linear and Vercel.

Why It Was Made

The core problem Loopline addresses is context-switching friction: teams using Jira for tickets, Notion for docs, Slack for communication, Figma for design — each with its own login, notifications, and workflow. The brief was to create a unified workspace that eliminates the need to jump between a dozen different applications.

The marketing site needed to communicate this value proposition instantly. The hero features a live, animated Kanban board that cycles cards every three seconds — a subtle but powerful demo that shows rather than tells. The pricing page includes a real toggle with calculated annual savings and a side-by-side comparison table. Every page is designed to reduce friction for adoption: clear CTAs, detailed feature documentation, and trust signals (SOC 2 compliance, AES-256 encryption, 99.99% SLA).

Who It Was Made For

The site targets technology and software product teams — specifically engineering managers frustrated by tool sprawl who want a single source of truth for tasks, docs, and timelines; startup founders and small business owners looking for an affordable all-in-one solution (free Starter plan, $29/mo Pro); and operations/IT decision-makers at larger organizations needing SSO, audit logs, and dedicated support ($99/mo Enterprise).

The design language and integrations (GitHub, GitLab, Figma, Linear, Slack) clearly signal a tech-forward audience of 2 to 200+ person teams. The FAQ section addresses specific objections: free plan limits, security certifications, data retention policies, and nonprofit discounts.
